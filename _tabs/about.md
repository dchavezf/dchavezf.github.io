### From Regional Leader to the Indifference of the Algorithm

![Daniel Chavez Flores](/resume/profile_image.jpg){: width="180" .right}

For nearly my entire career, I was an unbearable technical snob.

I was convinced that if my architecture was flawless and my queries flew, the market owed me success by sheer right. I despised HR, hated networking, and blindly believed that elegant code was the final product.

I was wrong. That technical arrogance was the biggest bug of my career.

I am Daniel Chávez Flores. I have spent over 22 years in the corporate trenches of Fortune 500 companies. I operate remotely from San Luis Potosí, Mexico, strictly on Central Standard Time without daylight saving time adjustments. Today, I position myself at the exact intersection where technical projects either die or scale: **Data Product Management & Enterprise Data Architecture**.

I discovered my true function the day I watched three C-level executives present three different revenue numbers in the same boardroom. The data pipeline worked perfectly, but the board was suffering from a massive *Definition Gap*. We built incredibly sophisticated and expensive pipes to carry water that absolutely no one in the company wanted to drink.

The board doesn't care about your code. The code is the medium; the financial certainty of the CFO is the actual product.

### My Engineering Manifesto

I am not a ticket-taker. I am the strategic architect who closes the gap between raw data execution and the boardroom. My job is uncertainty reduction.

* **Enterprise Architecture at Scale:** I led the corporate rescue and migration of 10TB of historical data for 20 LatAm legal entities into Snowflake. We did it with zero-downtime—rebuilding the kitchen while serving dinner.


* **Analytics Discovery & Product Management:** I transform chaotic regional requirements and data silos into a *Single Source of Truth*. I led product discovery for the LatAm BI Data Warehouse, packaging executive data stories for over 500 corporate users.


* **AI-Augmented Data Operations:** If your data governance is broken, an LLM is just a faster, more expensive GIGO 2.0 machine. I deploy metadata-driven RAG architectures (Claude, Python, FastAPI) strictly to automate deterministic workflows, accelerating development cycles by 80% without sacrificing compliance.


* **Systems, Not Dependencies:** If the system requires my physical presence to operate, it's not a system: it's a cage of labor dependency. I package raw human experience into parameterized engines that run consistently — which is why my engagements are scoped around outcomes and risk reduction, not hours on a clock.



### The Strategic Stack

The only true value of a Modern Data Stack (GCP, Snowflake, AWS, dbt, Airflow, PySpark) is delivery risk reduction. I champion immutable layer design (Data Vault 2.0), micro-partitioning to slash operational costs, and strict compliance with SOX audits for financial data.

Information is power, but only if it is a replicable system.

You are the product, not your code. The question isn't how much the data stored on your servers is worth. The real question is: **How much financial uncertainty and metric variance is your board willing to tolerate in the next quarter-end close?**
