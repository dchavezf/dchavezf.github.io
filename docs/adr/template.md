### **The Boardroom Hook**
Open with the business consequence behind the decision. Name the delivery pressure, margin risk, governance exposure, audit concern, stakeholder conflict, or executive trust problem that makes this architecture choice matter.

This section should make clear that the ADR is not documenting a preference. It is documenting a defensible response to a real operating constraint.

### **The Real Problem**
Describe the current state and why it is structurally insufficient.

Include the constraints that shaped the decision:

*   Business risk or stakeholder pressure.
*   Data, platform, or delivery scale.
*   Operational failure modes.
*   Governance, auditability, cost, or maintainability concerns.
*   Why the existing approach does not survive the next stage of delivery.

### **The ADR (The Decision)**
State the decision directly.

We are choosing **[selected approach]** over **[rejected approach]** because **[primary rationale]**.

**The Decision Drivers:**
*   **Driver one:** Explain the first reason in business terms first, then the technical mechanism.
*   **Driver two:** Explain the second reason with a concrete trade-off.
*   **Driver three:** Explain what this decision deliberately avoids.
*   **Driver four:** Explain the operating capability this decision creates.

### **The Replicable Engine**
Describe how the decision becomes a repeatable operating pattern rather than a one-off implementation.

1.  **Mechanism one:** What reusable process, code pattern, contract, test, or control makes this decision durable?
2.  **Mechanism two:** What improves delivery speed, quality, observability, or auditability?
3.  **Mechanism three:** What measurable business or engineering outcome should this enable?
4.  **Accepted trade-off:** What cost, limitation, or operational burden are we knowingly accepting?

### **The Closing**
Close by restating the leadership-level implication of the decision. The final paragraph should connect the technical choice back to trust, delivery risk, financial clarity, or reusable capability.

Optional final question: **What decision does this force leadership to make next?**
